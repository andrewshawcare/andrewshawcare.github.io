---
title: On the love of the wall
---

![Aletheia returns to Critias, no nearer an answer, but with a better question.](./art.png)

---

{% dialogue %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}I have been thinking about what you left me with.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}The thing you did not have an answer to.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}What makes the walking continue. Why a man who could lean on the map would lift his hand from it and put it back on the stone.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}And?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}I want to say: because he is honest. But honesty is a name for the thing I am trying to explain. It is not the explanation.{% /line %}
{% /dialogue %}

---

{% dialogue %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Begin lower. What does the slow man get from his touch that the runner does not?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}A correction, when he is wrong. A confirmation, when he is right.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}And which does he go for?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}...The confirmation. If I am honest. The hand reaches more eagerly toward the stone it expects than toward the stone it does not.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Then he does not love the wall. He loves being right about the wall.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}That is not the same thing.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}It is almost the opposite. A man who loves being right reaches where he expects, finds what he expected, and calls the corridor known. A man who loves the wall reaches where he does not expect, because that is where the wall might be that he has not yet met.{% /line %}
{% /dialogue %}

---

{% dialogue %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}Then the habit I am looking for is not a habit of touching. It is a habit of touching where one would rather not.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Where the map is thinnest. Where one's own confidence is loudest.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}You are making this into a virtue, and I am suspicious of virtues. A virtue is what we praise in a man when we cannot say how he came by it. If the answer is "the walker must love being stopped," I have not built anything. I have only described the man I wish I had.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}A fair complaint. Set the man aside, then. What does the touch itself give him, that nothing else gives?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}The world's own pushing-back. And it pushes back in two times. There is the pushing-back that comes when his hand has met the stone — the bruise, the stop, the corridor refusing him. That is the late teacher. It teaches by hurting.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}And the other?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}The pushing-back that comes before his hand has met anything. When he names what he is about to do, and the naming itself does not hold together. He has not moved — but the request, examined, contradicts itself. He wanted the wall to be where it is not. He wanted two stones in the same place. He wanted to arrive somewhere by a path that does not lead there.{% /line %}
{% /dialogue %}

---

{% dialogue %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}There is a teacher before the wall, and a teacher at the wall.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}Both are the same teacher, in the end. Both are the world refusing to be what it is not. But one refuses through the speech, and one refuses through the hand.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Name them.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}The first sits at the threshold. Before the agent moves, he asks: does what you are about to do answer to what is? If the request asks the world to be other than it is, he refuses it, and no step is taken. The second sits in the corridor. He cannot be consulted in advance. He answers only by being met.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}And neither alone is enough.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}The first catches requests that are wrong on their face — the ones that need no corridor to refute them. But many requests are coherent and still wrong. They could be true; they happen not to be. The first lets them through. Only the wall sends them back.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}And the second?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}Is late. By the time he answers, the step has been taken. If the step was cheap, the bruise is cheap. If the step was the kind that cannot be untaken — a stone moved, a man harmed, a thing said that cannot be unsaid — the bruise is the price of the lesson, and the lesson does not refund it.{% /line %}
{% /dialogue %}

---

{% dialogue %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}A pretty pair. I am going to dismantle one of them. Your first teacher — what is he, really? A man at a threshold, judging requests. By what?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}By what he knows of the corridor.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Which came from where?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}From walking it. From others walking it. From the map.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Then he is the map, sitting up and pretending to be a teacher. Everything he refuses, he refuses because the map says the corridor cannot be that way. But you have already told me the map is silent. The map can drift. The map can be wrong about the corridor and not know it. Your first teacher is the stele again, with a robe on.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}...{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}You wanted a teacher who could refuse a request before it was tried. The only ground he has to refuse on is what was learned by previous trying. He is borrowed authority. The moment the corridor changes, his refusals become superstitions, and his permissions become traps.{% /line %}
{% /dialogue %}

---

{% dialogue %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}Then I have lost him.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Have you? Or have you only learned what he is?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}He is yesterday's touching, used early. He cannot tell me whether the corridor is as he thinks; he can only tell me whether the request agrees with what the corridor used to be.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}So he is right most of the time, and wrong sometimes, and cannot tell which is which. He is the map again. There is no second teacher.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}There is no second teacher. There is the map, used early — at the threshold, before the step — and the map, used late, by walking. The same silent thing, consulted at two different prices.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}And you should have said that from the beginning.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}I gave him a name and a robe because I wanted there to be two of him. There is only one, and he is silent, and the only difference is whether I pay for his counsel in thought or in bruise.{% /line %}
{% /dialogue %}

---

{% dialogue %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}You have not yet answered me about the walker. Why does he keep walking? You have given him one silent teacher, consulted twice. You have not told me what makes him submit to him at all.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}I think the answer is in what we said and I did not see.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Say it.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}The walker keeps walking because he wants the request he is about to make to be one the corridor can answer. Not one the map can permit — the map can permit anything, given enough drift. One the corridor can answer.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}So he walks because he wants his speech to mean something.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}A request the corridor cannot answer is a request that means nothing. It is a noise shaped like a sentence. The walker who has stopped walking is making such noises and does not know it. He is using his mouth and the corridor is not in the room.{% /line %}
{% /dialogue %}

---

{% dialogue %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}I want to press once. You have given the walker a reason. A reason is not a force. Many men have reasons to do what they do not do.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}You are right.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}The runner also wishes his speech to mean something. He simply believes it already does. The difference between him and your walker is not in the wishing.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}It is in what they have been hurt by.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Say more.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}The runner has not yet been bruised by a wall he did not see. Or he has been, and has forgotten, or has called it bad luck. The walker has been bruised, and has not let the bruise become a story he tells about other men. He has kept the bruise as a thing that happened to him because the corridor was not what he thought, and he has come to expect to be wrong in that way again.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}So the habit is not virtue. It is a memory of a particular kind.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}A memory that one's own confidence has, before, been the shape of a wall not yet met.{% /line %}
{% /dialogue %}

---

{% dialogue %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Then your agent has a problem.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}It has the problem. An agent has no bruises. It has logs. A log is a writing. A writing is silent.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}The bruise that teaches the walker cannot teach the agent in the same way.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}The bruise teaches the walker because it hurt him. The log does not hurt the agent. The agent reads the log as it reads anything else — as marks, equally weighted, equally distant.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Then where in the agent does the memory live?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}It cannot live in the agent at all. The bruise has to live in me, and the agent has to be the shape my bruises have given me.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}You cannot install the walking. You can only build doubtful in the places you have been hurt, and confident in the places you have not, and the next bruise will come from the second.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}The agent does not learn. I learn, and rebuild it. The walking that keeps the map honest is mine.{% /line %}
{% /dialogue %}

---

{% dialogue %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Good. Now stop performing the realization and pay for it.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}What do you mean.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}You have said a heavy thing. The walking is yours, and cannot be subcontracted. So I ask you, now, before you leave: name a place where you have been running.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}...{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Not in general. Not for the agent. For yourself. Where, in the last month, did you ask the agent for a thing, take what it gave you, and not put your hand on the stone afterward?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}There is a piece of work I gave it three weeks ago. I read what came back. I judged it from the reading. I have not been to where the work was supposed to live. I do not know whether what I shipped is what I think I shipped.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}And?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}And I have shipped four more things on top of it.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Every wall you have not met is a wall you are still moving toward, faster than before.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}I know. You said it before, and I wrote it down, and I admired it, and I kept running.{% /line %}
{% /dialogue %}

---

{% dialogue %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}So. What will you do.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}I will go back to the first piece. I will put my hand on it. I will find what is there, not what I was told is there. And I will do this before I ship the next thing.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}You will not.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}What?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}You will not. You have just told me you have built a habit of running, and that the habit has run for three weeks past your noticing. A man does not unbuild such a habit by deciding to. He decides to a hundred times and runs the hundred and first. I am not blessing your resolution. I am telling you it is the runner's resolution — confident, untouched, made at speed.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}Then what is the use of saying it.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}The use is that you have said it where I can hear it, and where you cannot pretend you did not. When you ship the next thing without going back, you will know you are doing it. The bruise will not be that you ran. The bruise will be that you ran *after* you saw. And that bruise — if you keep it — is the only one that builds the walker.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}You are not going to let me leave on a good word.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}No. The good word is the colonnade. You have had three of those.{% /line %}
{% /dialogue %}

---

{% dialogue %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}Then I will go to the work.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Go.{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Aletheia" %}And if I find I have shipped what I did not mean to ship?{% /line %}
{% line speaker="Critias" %}Then you will have met the wall. And you will know, for the first time in three weeks, where you actually are.{% /line %}
{% /dialogue %}
