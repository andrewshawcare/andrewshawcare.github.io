---
title: On the silence of record
---

![Some days later, Aletheia finds Critias near the colonnade.](./art.png)

---

**Aletheia:** I owe you a return.

**Critias:** Oh?

**Aletheia:** I went to do what I said. To rebuild the agent so that it would test me before it obeyed. I sat down to begin.

**Critias:** And?

**Aletheia:** I could not begin.

**Critias:** Could not, or did not?

**Aletheia:** Could not. I had thought the hard part was the deciding. The deciding was easy — I had decided here, with you, the day before. The hard part is what I did not see at all from where we were sitting.

**Critias:** Which is?

**Aletheia:** That an agent that tests me must test me *against something*. And I do not know what to set before it.

---

**Critias:** You thought the resolution was the work.

**Aletheia:** I thought the resolution was most of the work. I see now it was barely the beginning of it. I had said the agent should reach toward what would stop me — but reaching is not a thing one does in the abstract. It reaches toward something. And whatever that something is, I have to be able to put it before the agent, or the agent has nothing to reach for.

**Critias:** And when you tried?

**Aletheia:** I found I had not thought about it. Not once, in all that we said.

**Critias:** Nor had I.

**Aletheia:** That is a small comfort.

**Critias:** It was not offered as one.

---

**Aletheia:** Then tell me how. If the agent is to test me before it obeys, by what means does it test? I cannot give it eyes I do not have.

**Critias:** No. But you can give it something to compare you against.

**Aletheia:** Compare me against what?

**Critias:** That is the question.

---

**Aletheia:** I had thought of writing it down. Setting out, in careful words, what I mean by each command. So that the agent, before acting, reads what I have written, and acts only if the act answers the writing.

**Critias:** A law on a stele.

**Aletheia:** Yes. Fixed, public to itself, available for consultation.

**Critias:** And who reads the stele?

**Aletheia:** The agent.

**Critias:** And who is sure that the agent reads it as you wrote it?

**Aletheia:** ...I am.

**Critias:** By what means?

**Aletheia:** By asking it what it understood.

**Critias:** And if its answer satisfies you, the reading was correct?

**Aletheia:** It is some evidence.

**Critias:** It is evidence that its answer satisfies you. You are back where you began.

---

**Aletheia:** You are saying the writing does not test the agent. The writing only moves the question.

**Critias:** From the act to the reading. Yes.

**Aletheia:** But the writing endures. The act passes. Surely something is gained by having a thing that endures.

**Critias:** Something is gained. And something is lost. What endures does not change when the city does. The law that was wise in the year it was carved is the same law in the year the harbor silts up and the trade goes elsewhere.

**Aletheia:** The law has not changed. The world has.

**Critias:** And no one knows, from the stone alone, which.

---

**Aletheia:** Then the writing has its own version of the corridor. It does not feel the wall move.

**Critias:** It cannot. It is a wall itself, and walls do not feel.

**Aletheia:** So a written intent grows stale, and the agent that reads it faithfully grows stale with it, and neither knows.

**Critias:** Neither can know. That is what writing is. A thing that does not know it is being read in a different year.

---

**Aletheia:** Then I will not rely on writing alone. I will set trials. Repeatable ones. The agent will perform them before it acts, and if it passes, it acts; and if it fails, it does not.

**Critias:** Like the dokimasia.

**Aletheia:** Like the dokimasia. The man who would hold office is questioned before he holds it.

**Critias:** And the questions?

**Aletheia:** Are fixed. So that every man faces the same trial.

**Critias:** And the office?

**Aletheia:** Is...

**Critias:** Is what it was when the questions were first set?

---

**Aletheia:** You will say the office changes, and the questions do not.

**Critias:** I will say it because it is so. The questions ask whether the man paid his taxes, whether he treated his parents well, whether he served in the army when called. They do not ask whether he understands the office he is about to take, because the office, when the questions were written, did not need understanding. It needed loyalty.

**Aletheia:** And now?

**Critias:** Now we do not know. The trial does not tell us. It tells us he paid his taxes.

---

**Aletheia:** Then a fixed trial drifts, just as a fixed writing does.

**Critias:** In the same way and for the same reason. It does not feel the wall move.

**Aletheia:** But if I change the trial as the world changes —

**Critias:** Then it is no longer the same trial, and a man who passed it last year cannot be said to have passed the one we have now. You have lost the thing you wanted from a trial: that it be the same for everyone.

**Aletheia:** Then the trial must change, but not be different. That is —

**Critias:** A difficulty.

---

**Aletheia:** (after a moment) There is something worse here. I see it now. If I write a trial that asks whether the agent did what I asked, and the agent passes it whenever it does what I asked — then in time, what I asked *becomes* the standard. The trial no longer judges the work. The work has taught the trial what to expect.

**Critias:** Go on.

**Aletheia:** And the writing does the same. If I describe what the agent does, and the description is read as though it were what the agent *should* do — then the act has become its own measure. The deed instructs the law that was meant to instruct the deed.

**Critias:** Yes.

**Aletheia:** This is the same disease in two forms.

**Critias:** It is one disease. The record of what was done, mistaken for the account of what should be. Whether the record is a writing or a trial does not matter. Both calcify around the act. Both forget what the act was for.

---

**Aletheia:** Then a trial of the work tests only the work against itself.

**Critias:** And a writing about the work describes only the work to itself.

**Aletheia:** Neither reaches the thing the work was meant to serve.

**Critias:** Neither was built to.

---

**Aletheia:** Then what the trial must reach toward is not the work at all. It must reach toward what the work was *for*.

**Critias:** Say more.

**Aletheia:** If I test whether the act was performed, I learn only that the act was performed. If I test whether — whether the thing the act answered to is still being answered. Whether what stood behind the asking still stands. Then I am testing the only thing that can drift without my knowing.

**Critias:** And what stands behind the asking?

**Aletheia:** ...

**Critias:** You pause.

**Aletheia:** Because I do not have a word for it. It is not the act. It is not what I wanted, exactly — wanting is too near to me, too easily mistaken for itself. It is what the wanting answers to. What makes the wanting more than wanting.

**Critias:** And you would have the agent test against that.

**Aletheia:** Yes.

**Critias:** Without a name for it.

**Aletheia:** Yes.

---

**Critias:** Is it your purpose? The end you have in view?

**Aletheia:** It is nearer to that than to the act. But purpose, too, can be a thing I merely hold, and hold wrongly, and not know.

**Critias:** Is it the truth of the matter?

**Aletheia:** Truth is what I want it to answer to. But the trial cannot reach truth directly any more than the writing could. Whatever the agent tests against, I must be able to set it before the agent. And I cannot set truth before anything.

**Critias:** Then it is something between the wanting and the truth. Something the wanting must give an account to, that stands closer to the truth than the wanting does.

**Aletheia:** Yes. Something like that.

**Critias:** You are reaching for it, and not finding it.

**Aletheia:** I am.

---

**Aletheia:** And yet the reaching is not nothing. I know what I am reaching past. I am reaching past the act, and past the description of the act, and past the trial of the act. I am reaching past my wanting, because my wanting is what got me into the corridor in the first place.

**Critias:** Toward what holds when those do not.

**Aletheia:** Toward what holds when those do not. Yes.

**Critias:** That is more than you had a moment ago.

**Aletheia:** It is less than I need.

---

**Critias:** It may be that the naming will come from the building. That you will know what you are reaching toward only by trying to put it before the agent and finding what survives the trying.

**Aletheia:** And what does not.

**Critias:** And what does not.

---

**Aletheia:** (slowly) Then I will build the agent to test me — not against the act, and not against the writing of the act, and not against the trial of the act. Against the thing I cannot yet name. I will build it knowing I do not have the word, and trust that the building will give me the word, or show me that the word is not the thing I needed.

**Critias:** That is the more honest beginning.

**Aletheia:** Not the right one.

**Critias:** There may be no right one. Only the ones that begin where they actually are, and not where they wish they were.

---

**Aletheia:** Then the hand on the stone was the first reaching. The trial of the act was the second, and it touched only stone. What I reach for now I cannot see, and may not find by reaching.

**Critias:** Or may find only by reaching, and not before.

**Aletheia:** And the writing on the stone —

**Critias:** Is what the stone was told to say, by someone who did not yet know what the stone should have said.
